OVERVIEW
--------
HarvestLink GmbH is a German agricultural supply chain platform founded in 2018,
headquartered in Munich. The company operates a B2B marketplace and traceability
platform connecting grain producers, grain traders, feed mills, and food processors
across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland (DACH region).

Using blockchain-backed lot tracking and AI-driven demand forecasting, HarvestLink
aims to reduce post-harvest losses and improve price discovery for grain producers.
The company generates revenue through transaction fees (0.4% of GMV) and SaaS
subscriptions for premium analytics.

STRATEGIC NOTES
---------------
- Revenue declined -10% YoY due to loss of two anchor customers (moved to in-house solution)
- CFO departure and headcount reduction (-35%) are significant negative signals
- Bridge round in 2024 was done at flat valuation — indicates investor hesitation
- Management is exploring a strategic sale; mandate given to a Frankfurt-based M&A boutique
- GMV growth (+8%) diverging from revenue decline suggests pricing pressure on take rate

KEY RISKS
---------
- Critical runway concern: 11 months without additional funding or strategic exit
- Revenue declining despite GMV growth — structural take rate compression risk
- CFO vacancy at a critical juncture is a major governance risk
- Customer concentration: top 3 customers represent ~60% of SaaS ARR
- Blockchain traceability: buyer interest has waned; technology may be overengineered for market

ANALYST PRELIMINARY VIEW
------------------------
HarvestLink is in financial distress. The combination of revenue decline, CFO departure,
headcount reduction, emergency bridge round, and 11-month runway points to a company
at a critical inflection point. While GMV growth is encouraging, the monetisation model
is under pressure. Not a priority investment candidate at current stage; monitor for
distressed acquisition opportunity if strategic sale process progresses.
