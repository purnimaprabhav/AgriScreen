PURPOSE
-------
This document defines the ESG scoring methodology applied by Pivot & Co when
evaluating agricultural companies for investment or strategic advisory mandates.
It is designed to be consistent with the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure
Regulation (SFDR) Article 8 and 9 product classification criteria, and aligned
with the TCFD recommendations for climate-related financial disclosure.

SCORING DIMENSIONS
------------------
ESG scores are assessed on a 0-100 scale across three pillars, equally weighted:

PILLAR 1 — ENVIRONMENTAL (0-100)
Criteria:
- Direct GHG emissions reduction per unit of output (scope 1+2)
- Water consumption efficiency improvement
- Pesticide / synthetic fertiliser reduction impact
- Biodiversity impact (soil health, pollinator protection, habitat)
- Climate resilience of core product/service in warming scenarios (+1.5°C, +2°C)
- Third-party environmental certification (ISO 14001, Rainforest Alliance, etc.)

Score benchmarks:
90-100 : Demonstrable, independently verified positive environmental impact
70-89  : Strong impact claims with partial verification
50-69  : Neutral / mixed impact; environmental benefits not core to model
30-49  : No material environmental differentiation
0-29   : Potential negative environmental externalities

PILLAR 2 — SOCIAL (0-100)
Criteria:
- Farmer income impact (does the product/service improve smallholder income?)
- Food security contribution (SDG 2 alignment)
- Labour practices (company's own workforce)
- Community engagement in operating regions
- Gender diversity in leadership team (>30% target)

Score benchmarks:
90-100 : Proven positive social impact with third-party assessment
70-89  : Strong social mission embedded in business model
50-69  : Standard employment practices; limited social differentiation
30-49  : Limited social impact data available
0-29   : Identified social risks (labour, community conflict)

PILLAR 3 — GOVERNANCE (0-100)
Criteria:
- Board independence and composition
- Founder equity concentration (>60% = risk flag)
- Financial reporting quality and frequency
- Key person risk mitigation
- Data privacy and cybersecurity governance (GDPR compliance)
- Anti-corruption and supply chain integrity

Score benchmarks:
90-100 : Best-in-class governance for stage; independent board, strong controls
70-89  : Adequate governance; minor gaps typical for stage
50-69  : Founder-controlled; basic controls in place
30-49  : Governance weaknesses identified (e.g., CFO vacancy, high concentration)
0-29   : Material governance failures or conflicts of interest

RED FLAGS (automatic score reduction)
--------------------------------------
The following factors trigger automatic pillar score reductions:
- Unverified ESG claims or greenwashing indicators : -20 points Environmental
- Ongoing regulatory investigation                : -15 points Governance
- >1 senior leadership departure in 12 months     : -10 points Governance
- Customer concentration >50% in top 3             : -10 points Governance
- Revenue decline with no structural explanation  : -10 points Social (employee risk)
- No independent board member at Series A+        : -15 points Governance

COMPOSITE ESG SCORE
-------------------
Composite = (Environmental + Social + Governance) / 3

Classification:
80-100 : ESG Leader — suitable for Article 9 fund mandates
65-79  : ESG Positive — suitable for Article 8 fund mandates
50-64  : ESG Neutral — standard screening; no mandate restriction
35-49  : ESG Caution — enhanced due diligence required
0-34   : ESG Risk — excluded from ESG-labelled mandates

MONITORING THRESHOLDS
---------------------
Automated alerts should be generated when:
- ESG composite score drops below 60
- Environmental pillar score drops below 55 (triggers enhanced monitoring)
- Governance red flag detected (any single red flag event)
- Annual ESG score delta (vs. prior year) exceeds -15 points

ALIGNMENT WITH SDGS
-------------------
All portfolio company monitoring tracks contribution to:
SDG 2  : Zero Hunger — primary mandate
SDG 6  : Clean Water — secondary (water management companies)
SDG 13 : Climate Action — primary mandate
SDG 15 : Life on Land — secondary (biodiversity/soil companies)

NOTE ON AI-ASSISTED SCORING
---------------------------
This framework is designed to be partially automated via NLP extraction from
company documents. However, all AI-generated ESG scores must be validated by
a human analyst before inclusion in investment committee materials.
Confidence thresholds: AI scores with <70% extraction confidence should be
flagged for manual review.
