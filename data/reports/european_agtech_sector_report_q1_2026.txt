EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
European AgTech investment reached EUR 2.1B in 2025, a 12% increase over 2024
despite broader venture market contraction. The sector continues to attract capital
driven by three macro tailwinds: (1) EU Farm to Fork strategy mandates, (2) input
cost inflation following the 2022 Ukraine conflict, and (3) accelerating climate
stress on agricultural productivity.

Precision agriculture, biological inputs, and agri-data infrastructure remain the
three largest sub-segments by deal count. Water management has emerged as the
fastest-growing sub-segment by capital deployed (+34% YoY) reflecting increasing
drought severity across Southern Europe and North Africa.

KEY INVESTMENT THEMES — 2026
----------------------------

1. BIOLOGICAL INPUTS & SOIL HEALTH
Regulatory tailwinds from the EU SUR (Sustainable Use Regulation) are accelerating
farmer adoption of biostimulants and biocontrol agents. The EU biostimulant market
is projected to reach EUR 1.8B by 2028 (CAGR 13%). Key dynamics:
- Bayer, Syngenta, and BASF all made strategic minority investments in biotech
  startups in 2025, signalling pre-emptive M&A positioning
- Carbon sequestration measurement demand is creating adjacent opportunities for
  soil health monitoring companies
- Registration timelines under EU FPR remain a key bottleneck (avg. 28 months)

2. PRECISION AGRICULTURE & AI
AI-powered farm management platforms are maturing. Revenue multiples have
compressed from 12x ARR (2021) to 6-7x ARR (2025). Differentiation increasingly
depends on proprietary datasets rather than algorithmic sophistication.
- Companies with >500,000 ha of monitored land command significant data moats
- Integration with ERP/agri-management systems (ISAGRI, Beekeeper, Trimble) is
  a critical determinant of customer retention (NRR of best-in-class: 118%)
- Satellite data democratisation (Sentinel, Planet) is commoditising basic NDVI;
  edge AI and in-field sensing are emerging as next differentiators

3. WATER MANAGEMENT
Southern European drought declarations (Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece) have
elevated water management to a Tier 1 priority for both farms and governments.
EU Water Framework Directive enforcement is tightening from 2026, requiring
farms >50 ha to file water use reports.
- Smart irrigation companies saw median revenue growth of +41% in 2025
- Israeli and Australian companies with water management expertise are expanding
  aggressively into European markets
- OCP Group (Morocco) partnership with irrigation companies signals Africa
  as the next high-growth geography

4. SUPPLY CHAIN & TRACEABILITY
Post-pandemic supply chain restructuring has increased farmer and buyer interest
in traceability solutions. However, monetisation remains challenging:
- Blockchain-based traceability has underdelivered on commercial expectations;
  most players pivoting to lighter-weight AI-driven lot tracking
- EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) creating urgency for commodity traceability
  in cocoa, soy, palm — spillover effects expected in European grains by 2027
- B2B marketplace models struggling with take rate compression as large buyers
  negotiate custom arrangements

RISK FACTORS
------------
- Venture funding slowdown: median deal size in agtech fell 18% in 2025 vs 2024
- EU SUR legislative uncertainty: partial withdrawal of pesticide reduction targets
  in 2025 created regulatory ambiguity for biological input companies
- Climate-related yield volatility: extreme weather events in 2025 (France floods,
  Iberian drought) disrupted customer operations and slowed procurement decisions
- AI regulation: EU AI Act Article 22 may require conformity assessments for
  autonomous agricultural decision-support systems above a certain risk threshold

OUTLOOK
-------
We expect 2026 to be a year of consolidation in European AgTech. Large agri-input
companies (Bayer, BASF, Syngenta, Nutrien) will continue acquiring or taking minority
stakes in precision ag and biological input companies. Water management will attract
the largest new investments. Supply chain and traceability companies face a difficult
funding environment; expect further distressed situations.

Recommended watchlist priorities for Pivot & Co:
HIGH  : Biological inputs (IP-heavy, regulatory moat), water management (southern Europe)
MED   : Precision agriculture (consolidating; look for data moat leaders)
LOW   : B2B marketplace / supply chain (monetisation risk)
