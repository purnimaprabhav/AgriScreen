[NEWS-001] — 14 January 2026
VERDANT FARMS SIGNS LETTER OF INTENT WITH CRÉDIT AGRICOLE
Verdant Farms SA has signed a non-binding Letter of Intent (LOI) with Crédit
Agricole's digital banking arm to integrate the AgroLens platform into the bank's
farmer advisory services. If converted to a binding agreement, the deal would give
AgroLens access to Crédit Agricole's 250,000 French farm customers. Verdant's CEO
described it as "a potential step-change in our distribution strategy." Financial
terms were not disclosed. The LOI is subject to a 90-day exclusivity window.
Source: Verdant Farms press release; AgriInvestor confirmation.

[NEWS-002] — 21 January 2026
EU FARM TO FORK: PESTICIDE REDUCTION TARGET PARTIALLY REINSTATED
Following political controversy in 2024, the European Parliament voted to reinstate
a 35% pesticide reduction target by 2030 (down from the original 50%). This provides
partial regulatory clarity for biological inputs companies. Analysts at Rabobank note
that this "reduces the upside scenario for biocontrol companies but confirms the
structural direction of travel." BioRoot Innovations and similar companies are
expected to benefit from continued regulatory tailwinds.
Source: European Parliament official press release; Rabobank Food & Agribusiness note.

[NEWS-003] — 3 February 2026
HARVESTLINK CFO RESIGNATION CONFIRMED
HarvestLink GmbH officially confirmed the resignation of CFO Thomas Richter
(effective 31 October 2025) in their latest Handelsregister filing. The company
stated it is "actively recruiting a replacement." Market sources indicate the
departure was related to disagreements over the company's fundraising strategy.
This follows the company's announcement in November 2025 of a headcount reduction
of 18 employees (35% of total workforce). HarvestLink's runway is estimated at
under 12 months by multiple sources.
Source: Handelsregister filing DE-M-2026-00234; AgFunder News.

[NEWS-004] — 11 February 2026
SOILSENSE AI SUBMITS VERRA VCS CARBON METHODOLOGY
SoilSense AI Ltd confirmed it has formally submitted its soil carbon quantification
methodology to Verra for VCS validation. If approved (expected Q3 2026), the
methodology would allow SoilSense to issue verified carbon credits on behalf of
participating farms. The CEO stated: "This is a transformational milestone. We
estimate the carbon credit module could add GBP 3-5M in annual revenue by 2027
if validated." Two competing methodologies are already validated (Gold Standard
Soil Carbon and BCarbon), but SoilSense claims its sensor-based approach is more
accurate and cost-effective.
Source: SoilSense AI press release; Verra VCS programme registry.

[NEWS-005] — 28 February 2026
GREENYIELD TECHNOLOGIES LAUNCHES COSUN PILOT IN NETHERLANDS
GreenYield Technologies BV announced the launch of a commercial pilot programme
with Cosun, one of the Netherlands' largest sugar beet cooperatives, covering
8,000 hectares across the Groningen and Zeeland growing regions. The pilot will
run through the 2026 growing season and evaluate CropGuard's early disease
detection system against Cosun's existing fungicide application regime.
Results will be published in Q4 2026. If the pilot meets agreed KPIs
(≥30% fungicide reduction without yield loss), Cosun has indicated it will
consider a broader commercial rollout across its full 180,000 ha member base.
Source: GreenYield Technologies press release; Cosun media centre.

[NEWS-006] — 15 March 2026
AQUAGROW SOLUTIONS PARTNERS WITH OCP GROUP ON MOROCCO EXPANSION
AquaGrow Solutions and OCP Group (the world's largest phosphate exporter and a
major Moroccan agricultural player) have signed a strategic partnership to deploy
AquaGrow's precision irrigation systems to 25,000 smallholder farms in the Souss-Massa
and Gharb regions of Morocco over three years. OCP will subsidise hardware costs as
part of its AgriEdge programme for smallholder modernisation. Financial terms of the
partnership were not disclosed, but market sources estimate the contract value at
USD 8-12M over the three-year period.
Source: AquaGrow Solutions press release; OCP Group annual sustainability report 2025.

[NEWS-007] — 22 March 2026
BIOROOT INNOVATIONS CONFIRMS SERIES A CLOSE AT EUR 12M
BioRoot Innovations SA confirmed the close of its EUR 12M Series A round, combining
a EUR 2.5M grant from the European Innovation Council (EIC Accelerator) and EUR 9.5M
equity from Swifty Ventures (lead) and Bayer AG Strategic Investments (follow-on).
The round values BioRoot at EUR 55M post-money. CEO Dr. Thomas Leclercq stated:
"This funding will allow us to complete our Spanish and Italian regulatory submissions,
scale RhizoBoost commercial operations, and begin Phase 2 trials for FungalRoot."
BioRoot's Bayer partnership gives it exclusive distribution rights in DACH and Benelux.
Source: BioRoot Innovations press release; EIC Accelerator official announcement.

[NEWS-008] — 31 March 2026
EU WATER FRAMEWORK DIRECTIVE: NEW REPORTING OBLIGATIONS FROM Q3 2026
The European Commission confirmed that farms exceeding 50 hectares in EU member
states will be required to file annual water use reports starting from Q3 2026,
under the strengthened Water Framework Directive enforcement regime. Farms in
water-stressed regions (Southern Spain, Southern Italy, parts of Greece and Portugal)
will face additional restrictions on groundwater extraction. Precision irrigation
companies are expected to benefit from increased demand for automated water use
reporting tools.
Source: European Commission DG Environment press release.
